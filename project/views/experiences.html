The Duquesne Incline is a funicular railway located near Pittsburgh's South Side neighborhood and scaling Mt. Washington. It was completed in 1877 and is 794 feet long, 400 feet in elevation, and has a grade of 30.5 degrees. The incline travels at a speed of 6.0 miles per hour and has a capacity of 18 persons per car. The incline was built for $47,000 in 1877 dollars and opened to the public on May 20th, 1877.
